# 🌍 PRIO (SDG) – Educational Games for Sustainable Development Goals

## 📌 Project Overview

PRIO (SDG) is a project focused on developing **educational mobile games that promote awareness about the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).**

The main idea is to create games that are **not only entertaining and fun but also educational and meaningful**. Through interactive gameplay, users—especially children and young learners—can understand important global challenges such as poverty, climate change, equality, and environmental protection.

The application was developed using **MIT App Inventor**, a visual programming platform that allows the creation of mobile applications using **block-based coding**.

---

# 🎯 Theme of the Project

The main theme of this project is:

> **To design games that combine entertainment with education, helping players learn about global issues and sustainable development goals in an engaging way.**

Instead of traditional learning methods, the project uses **game-based learning** to encourage awareness and responsible behavior.

---

# ❗ Problem Statement

The world today faces many serious challenges that affect people, societies, and the environment. Some of the major global issues include:

* Poverty
* Lack of quality education
* Gender inequality
* Climate change
* Water scarcity
* Environmental degradation
* Lack of justice and strong institutions
* Loss of biodiversity

Although governments and organizations have introduced many initiatives, these problems still exist in many parts of the world. Therefore, **raising awareness and educating younger generations is extremely important**.

---

# 🔍 Existing Solutions and Their Limitations

Many initiatives and programs already exist to address these global issues, but awareness and participation remain limited. Some examples include:

### 1️⃣ No Poverty

Over **10% of the global population still lives in extreme poverty**. Programs such as rural development schemes aim to reduce poverty, but the problem still persists in many regions.

### 2️⃣ Zero Hunger

Food assistance programs provide financial support for food. However, long-term solutions such as **employment and sustainable agriculture** are needed.

### 3️⃣ Quality Education

Government programs such as **Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan** aim to provide universal education, but awareness about the importance of education is still necessary.

### 4️⃣ Good Health and Well-Being

Programs like the **National Health Mission** focus on improving healthcare access. However, many people are still unaware of healthy lifestyles and preventive healthcare.

### 5️⃣ Gender Equality

Initiatives such as **Beti Bachao Beti Padhao** promote equal opportunities for girls. Despite progress, gender inequality continues in many areas.

### 6️⃣ Clean Water and Sanitation

Access to clean water and sanitation is essential to prevent diseases. Programs like the **Water Action Decade** highlight the importance of water management.

### 7️⃣ Affordable and Clean Energy

Renewable energy sources such as **solar, wind, and hydropower** help reduce environmental impact and energy shortages.

### 8️⃣ Decent Work and Economic Growth

Economic development requires sustainable employment opportunities and innovation.

### 9️⃣ Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

Innovation and technology can help societies develop better systems for transportation, communication, and production.

### 🔟 Reduced Inequalities

Many inequalities exist based on **age, gender, disability, race, or ethnicity**. Awareness and social inclusion are necessary to address these challenges.

### 1️⃣1️⃣ Sustainable Cities and Communities

Urban development must focus on **sustainability, disaster prevention, and environmental protection**.

### 1️⃣2️⃣ Responsible Consumption and Production

Sustainable production and responsible consumption help reduce environmental damage and waste.

### 1️⃣3️⃣ Life on Land

Forests and ecosystems are essential for biodiversity and environmental balance.

### 1️⃣4️⃣ Climate Action

Climate change affects every country. Global initiatives such as the **Paris Agreement** aim to reduce climate impact.

### 1️⃣5️⃣ Life Below Water

Oceans cover **70% of the Earth's surface**, but many marine ecosystems are polluted and threatened.

### 1️⃣6️⃣ Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions

Conflicts, corruption, and weak institutions threaten sustainable development.

### 1️⃣7️⃣ Partnerships for the Goals

Collaboration between governments, organizations, and communities is essential to achieve sustainable development.

# 💡 Proposed Solution

This project proposes **educational mobile games that raise awareness about SDG goals** in an engaging and interactive way.

### Key Ideas

* Develop **games based on real-world problems related to SDGs**
* Educate players about **sustainability, equality, and environmental protection**
* Use **interactive storytelling and gameplay mechanics** to explain consequences of harmful actions
* Encourage players to adopt **positive behaviors and responsible decision-making**

### Game Concepts

The games aim to teach values such as:

* Importance of **farming and food security**
* Access to **clean water and sanitation (SDG 6)**
* Maintaining **healthy lifestyles (SDG 3)**
* Promoting **peace, justice, and fairness (SDG 16)**
* Protecting **nature and biodiversity**

### Reward System

Players will earn **coins after completing each level**.
In the **No Poverty (SDG 1) game concept**, these coins can be converted into donations that support **orphanages and social causes**.
